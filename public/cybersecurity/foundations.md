# Core Concepts & CIA Triad

Welcome to Cybersecurity Fundamentals! This module covers the bedrock of information security.

## What is Cybersecurity?
Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. These attacks are usually aimed at accessing, changing, or destroying sensitive information; extorting money from users; or interrupting normal business processes.

## The CIA Triad
The CIA triad is a model designed to guide policies for information security within an organization.
- **Confidentiality:** Ensuring that data is accessible only to those authorized to have access.
- **Integrity:** Ensuring that data is accurate and reliable and has not been tampered with.
- **Availability:** Ensuring that data and resources are available to authorized users when needed.

## Types of Hackers
- **White Hat:** Ethical hackers who use their skills for good, helping organizations find and fix vulnerabilities.
- **Black Hat:** Malicious hackers who break into systems for personal gain or to cause harm.
- **Grey Hat:** Hackers who may break the law but often without malicious intent (e.g., finding a bug and reporting it without permission).

## Common Threats
- **Malware:** Malicious software (viruses, worms, trojans).
- **Phishing:** Fraudulent attempts to obtain sensitive information by disguising as a trustworthy entity.
- **Ransomware:** Malware that encrypts files and demands payment for the decryption key.
- **Social Engineering:** Manipulating people into giving up confidential information.
