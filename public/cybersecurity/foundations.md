# Core Concepts & CIA Triad

Welcome to Cybersecurity Fundamentals! This module covers the bedrock of information security.

## What is Cybersecurity?
Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. These attacks are usually aimed at accessing, changing, or destroying sensitive information; extorting money from users; or interrupting normal business processes.

## The CIA Triad
The CIA triad is a model designed to guide policies for information security within an organization.
- **Confidentiality:** Ensuring that data is accessible only to those authorized to have access. (e.g., Encryption, MFA)
- **Integrity:** Ensuring that data is accurate and reliable and has not been tampered with. (e.g., Hashing, Digital Signatures)
- **Availability:** Ensuring that data and resources are available to authorized users when needed. (e.g., Backups, Redundancy, DDoS protection)

## Security Principles
- **Least Privilege:** Granting users only the minimum access necessary to perform their jobs.
- **Defense in Depth:** Using multiple layers of security controls to protect data.
- **Zero Trust:** Never trust, always verify. Every request must be authenticated and authorized.
- **Separation of Duties:** Dividing tasks among multiple people to prevent fraud or error.

## Types of Hackers
- **White Hat:** Ethical hackers who use their skills for good, helping organizations find and fix vulnerabilities.
- **Black Hat:** Malicious hackers who break into systems for personal gain or to cause harm.
- **Grey Hat:** Hackers who may break the law but often without malicious intent (e.g., finding a bug and reporting it without permission).
- **Script Kiddies:** Unskilled individuals who use existing scripts or tools to launch attacks.
- **State-Sponsored Hackers:** Hackers employed by governments to conduct cyber espionage or sabotage.

## Common Threats
- **Malware:** Malicious software (viruses, worms, trojans, spyware, adware).
- **Phishing:** Fraudulent attempts to obtain sensitive information by disguising as a trustworthy entity (Spear Phishing, Vishing, Smishing).
- **Ransomware:** Malware that encrypts files and demands payment for the decryption key.
- **Social Engineering:** Manipulating people into giving up confidential information (Tailgating, Baiting, Pretexting).
- **Man-in-the-Middle (MitM):** An attacker intercepts communication between two parties.
- **DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service):** Overwhelming a system with traffic from multiple sources to make it unavailable.

## Security Models
- **Bell-LaPadula:** Focuses on confidentiality (No Read Up, No Write Down).
- **Biba:** Focuses on integrity (No Read Down, No Write Up).
- **Clark-Wilson:** Focuses on integrity through separation of duties and well-formed transactions.
