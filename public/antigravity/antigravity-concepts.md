# Concepts Related to "Antigravity"

Antigravity is a hypothetical phenomenon of creating a place or object that is free from the force of gravity. It does not refer to the lack of weight under gravity experienced in free fall or orbit, or to balancing the force of gravity with some other force, such as electromagnetism or aerodynamic lift.

## Theoretical Approaches
- **Negative Mass:** A hypothetical concept of matter whose mass is of opposite sign to the mass of normal matter. According to general relativity, negative mass would produce a repulsive gravitational field.
- **Gravitational Shielding:** A hypothetical process of shielding an object from the influence of a gravitational field.

## Science Fiction vs. Reality
While antigravity is a staple of science fiction, there is currently no accepted scientific theory that allows for its existence. However, the study of theoretical physics continues to explore the boundaries of our understanding of gravity.

### Key Concepts
- **Biefeld-Brown Effect:** An electrical phenomenon that produces an ionic wind that transfers its momentum to surrounding neutral particles. Often mistakenly attributed to antigravity.
- **Acoustic Levitation:** A method for suspending matter in a medium by using acoustic radiation pressure from intense sound waves in the medium. This is not antigravity, but a balancing force.
